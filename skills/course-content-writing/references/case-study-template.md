# Case Study Template

```
## Case Study: [Title]
**Course:** [GCPP / MIPP / Executive / Other]
**Session:** [Session number and topic]
**Duration:** [How long to allocate for case discussion]
**Concept(s) illustrated:** [What analytical framework(s) this case teaches]

---

### Background

[Factual account of the policy situation — no analysis. 300–500 words.

Include:
- The policy problem or decision faced
- The institutional context (which ministry, which level of government, what the legal/regulatory framework was)
- The key actors and their formal roles
- The timeline: when did this happen, what were the key dates
- What was at stake

Do NOT editorialize. Write: "The Finance Ministry proposed X" — not "The Finance Ministry controversially proposed X". Participants will form their own analytical judgments.]

---

### The Decision Point

[One specific moment where a decision had to be made. Who had to decide? What were the options? By when? What information did they have — and what didn't they know?

This is the analytical core of the case. Write it clearly and specifically.]

---

### The Actors

| Actor | Role | Formal position on the issue |
|-------|------|------------------------------|
| [Ministry/Body] | [What they do] | [Their stated position — factual] |
| [...] | [...] | [...] |

---

### Discussion Questions

*These are for facilitated discussion — not for participants to write answers to.*

1. **[Analysis question]**: Apply [concept from course] to this case. What does it reveal about [aspect of the situation]?

2. **[Comparison question]**: How does this case compare to [another case from the course or widely known]? What accounts for the difference?

3. **[Critique question]**: What is the strongest argument against the decision that was made (or proposed)? Is that argument answered in the evidence we have?

4. **[Application question]**: If you were advising [key actor] at [decision point], what would you recommend — and what would you explicitly NOT recommend?

5. **[Synthesis question]**: How does this case change (or not change) your view of [concept or claim from a previous session]?

---

### Assessment Question (if used for graded work)

[One analytical question suitable for an essay or short answer. Must require application of course concepts — cannot be answered by summarising the case facts.

Example: "Using [framework], analyse why [outcome] occurred in this case. What does your analysis suggest about the conditions under which [similar outcomes] are likely?"]

---

*TEACHING NOTES — Instructor Only*

**What this case teaches:**
[What analytical insight should participants leave with? State it in one sentence.]

**Most important things to draw out in discussion:**
1. [...]
2. [...]
3. [...]

**Common errors participants make:**
[What most participants get wrong or miss when they first discuss this case]

**What actually happened:**
[If the case is based on a real episode: what was the actual decision, and what were the consequences? Include this information to share at the END of discussion — not before.]

**Connection to theory:**
[Which specific reading or concept from the course does this case best illustrate? How?]

**Follow-up cases:**
[One or two related cases that could extend this discussion in a future session]
```
