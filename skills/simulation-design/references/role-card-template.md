# Role Card Template

```
## Role Card: [Actor/Institution Name]
[CONFIDENTIAL — do not share with other participants]

### Who You Are
[One paragraph: institution name, mandate, where you sit in the policy system. Write in second person ("You are the Joint Secretary in charge of...")]

### Your Primary Objective
[The single outcome you most want from this negotiation. Be specific: not "a good outcome for your ministry" but "approval of the PLI scheme with a ₹X crore budget and MeitY as nodal ministry".]

### Your Secondary Objective
[What you would accept if your primary is unachievable. This should be genuinely different — not just a watered-down version of primary.]

### Your Red Lines
[What you cannot agree to under any circumstances. Give institutional or political reasons — not just "you don't want to". Red lines must be hard constraints, not preferences.]

1. [Red line 1] — because [institutional/political reason]
2. [Red line 2] — because [...]

### What You Know (that others don't)
[One or two pieces of private information that are material to the negotiation. Other actors do not know this. Use it strategically.]

- [Private information 1]
- [Private information 2]

### Your BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement)
[What happens to you if no agreement is reached? This should be genuinely different from your primary objective — it may be better or worse. An actor with a good BATNA has less incentive to compromise; an actor with a bad BATNA should be willing to make concessions.]

### Suggested Opening Move
[How should you open the negotiation? One or two sentences. Should signal your primary objective while not immediately revealing your red lines or private information.]

### Questions You Might Be Asked (and how to handle)
[2–3 questions other actors are likely to ask you, and how to respond without revealing too much.]
```
