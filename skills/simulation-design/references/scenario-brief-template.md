# Scenario Brief Template

*Shared with all participants before the simulation begins. Maximum 2 pages.*

```
## [Simulation Title]

### The Situation

[Describe the policy problem in 200–300 words. Be factual — no editorialising. Include:
- What triggered this situation (a policy proposal, a crisis, a deadline, a court order)
- Why it matters (what is at stake for the country, for the institutions involved)
- Who is in the room today and why (which actors are negotiating and what their formal roles are)
- The status quo (what happens if no decision is made)]

### The Decision

[State the exact decision the group must reach — specific and action-forcing. Examples:
- "Approve, reject, or modify the proposed PLI scheme. If approve: specify the budget, the nodal ministry, and the eligibility criteria."
- "Agree on a joint recommendation to the Cabinet on the data localisation clause of the Digital India Act. The recommendation must be a single position — not a list of options."
- NOT: "Discuss semiconductor policy" or "Find common ground on data governance"]

### Time Available

You have [X] minutes. A decision (or a documented impasse) must be produced at the end.

### Format

[Describe the format: 
- Single-group negotiation (all actors in one room)
- Multi-round (brief description of rounds)
- Committee hearing (some actors testify; committee decides)
- Crisis management (rolling developments introduced by facilitator)]

### Background: Key Facts

[Optional section — only if participants need specific data to play their roles. Keep brief: 3–5 bullet points of facts. If a fact is role-specific (private information), it belongs in the role card, not here.]

- [Fact 1]
- [Fact 2]
- [Fact 3]

### Note on Ambiguity

Some aspects of your role and this situation are deliberately ambiguous. This reflects reality: policy decisions are made with incomplete information, unclear mandates, and competing interpretations of the rules. Use your judgment.
```
